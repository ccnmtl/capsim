    Background

    The Public Health Capstone Simulation is a web-based learning environment for practicing the application of systems thinking to public health policy and intervention decision-making. The program takes participants through a series of systems thinking exercises that leads to the use of an agent-based simulation for testing relationship theories, and then applying findings to support an intervention proposal for addressing a public health challenge provided by the system. A successful intervention proposal requires the application of a systems perspective to approaching complex problems. The proposal consists of an evidence-based rationale that includes data generated by participant use of the simulation model, and requires the participant to back their intervention proposal with data coming from actual public health research.

    Partners
